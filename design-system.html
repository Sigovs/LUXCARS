Overview

Reference discipline

These luxury references inform discipline, not identity.

Borrow the principles, never the identity. We study how mature luxury systems hold their line: restraint, consistency, hierarchy, accessibility, typography discipline, photography quality, and tone control. Lux Cars Chicago stays itself.

  • Do not copy Ferrari, Bentley, Rolls-Royce, or Rolls-Royce SMR colors.
  • Do not copy their logos, monograms, marks, or layouts.
  • Do not copy their proprietary photography or icons.
  • Borrow only the principles listed above.

One accent hue. Zero blue. #8B2828 is primary, #742020 is hover, #5C1919 is pressed. Lighter tints (50 to 300) are for subtle backgrounds and states; deeper tints (800, 900) are for large fills and overlays over the dark base.

Metallic accent

Platinum 300#D8DBDE--platinum-300
Platinum 400#A9AEB4--platinum-400
Platinum 500#7C8288--platinum-500

Decorative metallic only. Never a CTA color.

Platinum stays sparse and thin. Used for hairline highlights and focus rings (300), icon accents, thin dividers, review stars, the LUX "X" mark and text-link hover (400), and pressed metallic details (500). It is cooler than the warm base on purpose. Not allowed on primary buttons, CTA backgrounds, large fills, badges, price emphasis, or major navigation. Actions stay oxblood red.

Foundations

Color usage ratio

Proportion is the luxury signal. Keep the base and negative space dominant; let red and platinum stay rare.

Warm-neutral basemin 60%

Page and section backgrounds, card surfaces, footer, main dark structure, and negative space.

Oxblood red8% to 12%

Primary CTA, active nav, key action states, selected emphasis only. The only action color.

Platinum3% to 6%

Hairline dividers, review stars, focus rings, the LUX "X", subtle icon strokes, text-link hover. Decorative only.

Text and muted neutralsremaining balance

Body copy, labels, metadata, and supporting UI. Not a secondary palette.

Do
  • Keep at least 60% base or negative space in every layout.
  • Let red carry one action per section.
  • Keep platinum thin: hairlines, stars, focus, the "X".
Don't
  • Turn platinum into a second CTA color.
  • Treat supporting tints as a secondary palette.
  • Add extra accent colors, blue, or gold.

Foundations

Content style

Direct, premium, calm, trustworthy, human, and simple. Authoritative without sounding bossy. If it sounds like an ad, rewrite it.

Direct

GoodSearch our hand-picked inventory.

BadDiscover unbeatable deals today!

Premium

GoodCarefully selected luxury vehicles.

BadHuge savings on luxury rides!

Calm

GoodGet a clear financing path before you visit.

BadApply now before it's gone!

Trustworthy

GoodInspected, documented, and ready for delivery.

BadGuaranteed approval for everyone!

Human

GoodWe'll help you review the vehicle, numbers, and next steps.

BadCustomers will be provided with multiple financing solutions.

Simple

GoodSchedule a private appointment.

BadInitiate a personalized vehicle acquisition consultation.

Do
  • One key message per section.
  • Short headings. Short sentences.
  • Plain words over jargon.
Don't
  • Overly clever luxury copy.
  • Anything that sounds like an ad or sounds desperate.
  • Three adjectives where one works.

No cheap dealer language. No aggressive urgency. No fake exclusivity. No bad-credit tone.

Quality

Luxury hygiene

The rules that keep the site out of cheap-dealership territory.

No distressed pricing

Do
  • View curated inventory
  • Request details
  • Get a clear number
Don't
  • Huge savings, discounted, price reduced
  • Special offer, was / now pricing
  • Blowout, manager special

No desperate inventory language

Do
  • Hand-picked inventory
  • Carefully selected vehicles
  • Updated collection
Don't
  • Large inventory, huge selection
  • Immediate delivery, in stock now
  • Must go, limited time only

No fake exclusivity

Do
  • Private appointments available
  • Curated luxury vehicles
  • A more personal buying experience
Don't
  • VIP only, ultra exclusive deals
  • Rare chance today
  • Once in a lifetime offer

No visual clutter

Do
  • One clear message per section
  • One primary CTA per section
  • Clean imagery, strong negative space
Don't
  • Multiple badges, sticker-style labels
  • Heavy overlays, too many CTAs
  • Crowded cards

No badge abuse

Do
  • Small labels only when useful
  • Keep badges quiet and functional
Don't
  • Oversized certification badges
  • Random trust seals, sticker graphics
  • Report-style visual clutter
